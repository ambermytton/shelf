# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Personal use — a single reader/viewer who wants a quiet, considered place to track books, films, and shows. Uses it in the evening, on a dark screen, in a focused mindset. Not a social product; not a recommendation engine. Deeply personal, like a well-worn notebook.

## Product Purpose

Shelf is a personal media tracking app. You add titles you want to read/watch, track what you're in the middle of, and log what you've finished. A taste profile computes "fit verdicts" (good/partial/poor) for each title based on your preferences. The app stays empty by default — it earns its data over time.

## Brand Personality

Considered. Quiet. Cinematic.

The feel should be like a personal library at night — dark, warm-lit, textured. Not a SaaS productivity tool. Not a streaming service. A personal, slightly literary object.

## Anti-references

- Letterboxd (too social, too orange, too playful)
- Goodreads (dated, cluttered, loud)
- Generic SaaS dashboards (white, blue pill buttons, card grids, data everywhere)
- Notion clones (too much chrome, too much structure)

## Design Principles

1. **The shelf earns its look** — the grid is the experience. Cards should feel like physical objects: cover art, texture, scale. Empty states should feel like an invitation, not an error.
2. **Quiet by default, rich on demand** — metadata hides until hover. Controls recede. The content is the UI.
3. **Considered over complete** — every element earns its place. No decorative sections, no filler cards, no eyebrows on everything.
4. **Dark is the native state** — the dark theme is the design, not a toggle. Canvas #0d0d0d, surface #1c1c1c.
5. **Personal scale** — this is for one person. Density and delight matter more than scalability and enterprise-safe spacing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Focus-visible rings required. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Panel has a focus trap. Reduced motion respected.
